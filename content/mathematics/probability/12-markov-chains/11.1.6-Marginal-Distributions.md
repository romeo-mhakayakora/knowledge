## The Initial Vector t

### Scouting Report
**What to look for:**
A row vector $t = (t_1, t_2, \ldots, t_M)$ where each component $t_i$ represents the probability of starting in state $i$.
**Why you care:**
This vector encodes your uncertainty about the system's initial conditions, which determines how all future probabilities propagate.

---

**The Initial Vector** $t = (t_1, t_2, \ldots, t_M)$ is the **spawn distribution**, where:

$$
t_i = P(X_0 = i)
$$

This is a $1 \times M$ row vector. Instead of saying "the agent starts in State 1," you say "the agent starts with a 50% chance of being in State 1 and a 50% chance of being in State 2":

$$
t = [0.5,\ 0.5,\ 0.0]
$$

## LOTP — The Law of Total Probability

### Scouting Report
**What to look for:**
A summation involving initial probabilities $t_i$ and conditional transition probabilities $P(X_n = j \mid X_0 = i)$.
**Why you care:**
This law resolves uncertainty about the starting state by averaging over all possible origins.

---

To find the **marginal** (absolute, unconditioned) probability of being in state $j$ at time $n$:

$$
P(X_n = j) = \sum_{i=1}^{M} P(X_0 = i) \cdot P(X_n = j \mid X_0 = i) = \sum_{i=1}^{M} t_i \cdot q_{ij}^{(n)}
$$

**Read out loud in English:**
> "The probability that $X_n$ equals $j$... equals the sum from $i$ equals one to $M$... of the probability that $X_0$ equals $i$... times the probability that $X_n$ equals $j$, **given that** $X_0$ equals $i$."

This summation is the **weighted average** over all possible starting timelines.

---

## The Battle Royale Intuition

**Core intuition:**
You take the probability of moving from $i$ to $j$, then **multiply** by the probability of starting there in the first place. You cannot just look at survival rates in a vacuum — Zone 3 has a 90% rate but nobody spawned there, so it contributes nothing.

## Proposition 11.1.6 — Full Proof Decomposed

### Scouting Report
**What to look for:**
A vector-matrix multiplication pattern $tQ^n$ where $t$ is a row vector and $Q^n$ is the $n$-step transition matrix.
**Why you care:**
This result lets you fast-forward uncertainty propagation through time without tracking every intermediate state.

---

### Proposition
Define $t = (t_1, t_2, \ldots, t_M)$ by $t_i = P(X_0 = i)$, viewed as a row vector. Then the marginal distribution of $X_n$ is given by the vector $tQ^n$. That is, the $j$-th component of $tQ^n$ is $P(X_n = j)$.

$$
P(X_n = j) = \sum_{i=1}^{M} P(X_0 = i) \cdot P(X_n = j \mid X_0 = i) = \sum_{i=1}^{M} t_i \cdot q_{ij}^{(n)}
$$

which is the $j$-th component of $tQ^n$ by definition of matrix multiplication. $\blacksquare$

---

### Metadata Block
| Element | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Structural Signature** | A summation over initial states weighted by transition probabilities |
| **Core Invariant** | The total probability mass remains normalized to 1 across all states |
| **Boundary / Failure Mode** | Fails if the initial vector isn't properly normalized or if transition probabilities don't sum to 1 |
| **Compression Handle** | "Spawn-weighted timeline average" |
| **Phenomenon Web** | Generalizes the single-start case by convex combination of all possible origins |

---

## Reading the Proof Out Loud in English

> "To find the total probability of ending up in state $j$ at time step $n$ ($P(X_n = j)$) — we sum up ($\Sigma$) the parallel timelines for every possible starting state $i$ — by multiplying the probability that we actually spawned in state $i$ ($P(X_0 = i)$) — by the conditional probability of reaching state $j$ given that we started in $i$ ($P(X_n = j \mid X_0 = i)$). This is mathematically identical to taking the $i$-th element of our starting vector ($t_i$) and multiplying it by the $i,j$ entry of our $n$-step transition matrix ($q_{ij}^{(n)}$) — which, by definition, is exactly how you calculate the $j$-th component of a vector-matrix dot product ($tQ^n$)."

---

### Conceptual Trap ⚠️
**Common Mistake:**
Assuming the initial vector $t$ is uniform (equal probabilities) when the system might have biased spawning.
**What goes wrong:**
Your marginal probabilities will systematically underestimate or overestimate actual system behavior based on the true spawn distribution.

## Symbol-by-Symbol Reference

| Symbol | Professor Speak | Plain English |
|--------|------------------|---------------|
| $P(X_n = j)$ | "The probability that $X$ sub $n$ equals $j$" | The absolute probability that the agent ends up in State $j$ exactly $n$ steps from now. |
| $\sum_{i=1}^{M}$ | "The sum, from $i$ equals one to $M$, of..." | Run a loop through every possible starting state and add the results together. |
| $P(X_0 = i)$ or $t_i$ | "The probability that $X$ sub zero equals $i$" | The chance the agent originally spawned in State $i$. |
| $P(X_n = j \mid X_0 = i)$ | "The probability that $X_n$ equals $j$, **given that** $X_0$ equals $i$" | Assuming the agent started in State $i$, what are its odds of reaching State $j$? |
| $q_{ij}^{(n)}$ | "q sub $i$-$j$, superscript $n$" | Go to our $n$-step matrix $Q^n$, find the number in row $i$, column $j$. |
| $tQ^n$ | "The vector $t$ times the matrix $Q$ to the $n$-th power" | Take the starting reality vector and crash it through our fast-forwarded probability matrix via a dot product. |
